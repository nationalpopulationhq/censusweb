Census Workflow Overview

The census workflow involves a structured process to ensure accurate and efficient data collection. The National Population Commission oversees the entire process, with various committees and coordinators managing specific tasks. Key Components of the Census Workflow

The workflow starts with the National Population Commission and flows through various levels of management and coordination. The Chief Technical Adviser (CTA) and technical advisers provide guidance and support. The Census Technical Working Group and Census Management ensure that the process is on track and adheres to the required standards.

At the regional level, zonal coordinators and state field coordinators oversee the operations in their respective areas. Local Government Area (LGA) field coordinators manage the fieldwork, supported by data quality assistants and supervisors who monitor the enumerators collecting data.

Several specialized committees, such as the National Census Publicity and Advocacy Committee, Census National Technical Advisory Committee, Census National Logistics Committee, and Census National Security Committee, provide additional support and guidance to ensure the success of the census.
